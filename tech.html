Your Consumer Rights: What You Need to Know

Understanding federal laws that protect your credit rights is essential. As a licensed credit services organization, FRS Credit Repair operates in full compliance with all regulations:

Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)

  • Access to free credit reports from each bureau annually
  • Right to dispute incomplete or inaccurate information
  • Time limits on negative information (typically 7 years)
  • Identity theft protections and fraud alerts
  • Medical debt changes (as of January 2025)

Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA)

  • Right to written contract detailing services
  • Three-day cancellation right
  • No payment before services are rendered
  • Protection against false claims and misleading practices
  • Right to sue companies that violate CROA

Professional Services vs. DIY Credit Repair

You Can Do It Yourself:

Federal law gives you the right to dispute errors on your credit reports for free. You can obtain free credit reports weekly at AnnualCreditReport.com and send dispute letters directly to credit bureaus.

DIY is best for: Simple errors, single inaccuracies, or those with time and patience to manage the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does professional credit repair take?

The time varies from a few months to several years, depending on the complexity of your credit issues. Simple errors might be resolved in 30-60 days, while more serious financial difficulties can take longer. Most negative items remain on your report for seven years, though their impact lessens over time. Professional services often achieve faster results than DIY approaches.

Can accurate negative information be removed?

Generally, no. Legitimate credit repair companies cannot legally remove accurate and current negative information from your credit report. However, we can help ensure all information is reported correctly and guide you in building positive credit habits to offset past issues.
